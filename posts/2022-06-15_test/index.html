The Lindblad master equation is a Markovian, first-order differential equation for calculating the time-evolution of a density matrix, \(\rho\).

$$ \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} = -\frac{i}{\hbar} \left[ H, \rho \right] + \sum_{i = 1}^{N^2 - 1} \gamma_i \left( L_i \rho L_i^\dagger - \frac{1}{2} \overline{ \left[ L_i^\dagger L_i, \rho \right] } \right) , $$ where \( \overline{[A, B]} \) is the anticommutator.1
