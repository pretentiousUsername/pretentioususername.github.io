In the begining

Yeah—that’s a real imaginative title. No one else has ever ripped of the Bible.

Behold!

Anyways, this is my new blog. I’m pretty sure absolutely no one in their right mind would want to read it, but I like writing, and I figured it’d be nice to put it all in one place.

Many people, as far as I know, like to keep blogs for employability’s sake—write about something in programming or whatnot—but for me, I just need a place to do some long-form writing. Social media sucks—I certainly wouldn’t want to use a site like Facebook for writing—so a Hugo blog it is.

Hugo isn’t very good

Not saying I could do better, but Hugo isn’t very good—or, at the very least, there aren’t many good themes for it; most static site generators aren’t very good.

Do you know how hard I hunted to find a new static site generator? I spent a whole hour trying to find one, and ended up with Hugo. It was either Hugo, dealing with javascript,1 or using something ancient.

Perhaps I simply do not understand how static site generators work, but I find working on Hugo stuff to be mind-boggling. So many themes tend to feel overcomplicated and almost impossible to edit well—even the erroneously named “shortcodes” people implement tend to be much longer, and much more easy to forget, than Markdown syntax.

That said, it’s still faster than the alternatives, and I don’t need to use javascript—that’s a plus, and likely the only reason I’m using Hugo.

All that said, I can’t complain much; Hugo is much more customizable than something like Squarespace or something like that, my site is hosted for free on Github—and, best of all, it’s not WordPress.

  1. You may also know javascript as the worst programming language known to humankind. ↩︎
